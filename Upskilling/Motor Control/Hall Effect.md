The Hall effect is the phenomenon where a voltage develops across a conductor or semiconductor when a current flows through it in the presence of a perpendicular magnetic field.

- Current flows through a conductor. A magnetic field is applied perpendicular to the current.
- The magnetic field exerts a Lorentz force on moving charge carriers (electrons or holes), pushing them to one side of the material. This separation of charges creates a measurable voltage across the sides, called the Hall voltage.
