
An encoder is a sensor that converts motion (like rotation or position) into electrical signals (it encodes into a signal). It’s commonly used in motors to measure speed, direction, or position for control systems.

This is an example of an encoder that goes on the back of a DC motor.

Actually, this example is both a driver and encoder. Two wires drive the motor, two drive the encoder and two pull information from the encoder.
