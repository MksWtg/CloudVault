A VFD, or Variable Frequency Drive, is an electronic device that controls the speed and torque of an AC motor by adjusting the frequency and voltage of the electricity supplied to it.

AC motors normally run at a speed determined by the supply frequency (e.g., 60 Hz in the US). A VFD can change that frequency, allowing the motor to spin faster or slower than the nominal speed. It also adjusts the voltage in proportion to the frequency to maintain proper torque.

Key points:

- Input: usually DC from a rectified AC source or DC supply.
- Output: controlled three-phase AC with adjustable frequency and voltage.

Essentially, a VFD acts like a smart inverter that lets you precisely control an AC motor’s speed, improving efficiency and reducing mechanical stress.
