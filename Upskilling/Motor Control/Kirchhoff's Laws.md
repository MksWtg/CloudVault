Kirchhoff's laws (KCL and KVL) are fundamental principles for circuit analysis based on the conservation of charge and energy.

## Node
A node is a junction point in an electrical circuit where two or more circuit elements (such as resistors, capacitors, or voltage sources) connect.
![[Pasted image 20260313210841.png]]

## KCL
The algebraic sum of all currents entering and leaving a node must equal zero.

![[Pasted image 20260313210850.png]]

## KVL
The algebraic sum of all voltage drops and electromotive forces (EMFs) around any closed network is zero.
