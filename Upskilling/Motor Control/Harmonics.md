A harmonic is a voltage or current in an electrical system that has a frequency that is an integer multiple of the fundamental frequency.

Fundamental frequency is the main frequency you want in your system for example, in a 50 Hz AC supply, 50 Hz is the fundamental. In a motor, this is the frequency of the desired sinusoidal voltage.

Harmonics are extra unwanted waves that appear on top of the fundamental.

They have frequencies like 2×, 3×, 4× the fundamental.

So for a 50 Hz system:

- 2nd harmonic = 100 Hz
- 3rd harmonic = 150 Hz
- 4th harmonic = 200 Hz, etc.
