An inverter is an electronic device that converts DC (direct current) electricity into AC (alternating current) electricity.

In motor control, an inverter usually takes a DC voltage from a battery or power supply and switches it through transistors in a controlled way to create AC waveforms for driving a three-phase motor like a BLDC or PMSM. By controlling the timing and amplitude of the switches, the inverter can control motor speed, torque, and direction.

Inverters are used in many applications:

- Electric vehicles: converting the battery’s DC to AC for the motor
- Solar power systems: converting solar panel DC to AC for the grid
- Variable frequency drives: controlling AC induction motors in industry

In short, an inverter is what allows DC power to drive AC motors and enables precise motor control by adjusting the waveform.
