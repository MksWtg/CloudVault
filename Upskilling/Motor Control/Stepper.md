Stepper motors are their own category of motor. They are not simply BLDC, PMSM, AC induction, or brushed DC motors, although they share some similarities with brushless permanent-magnet motors.

They are most closely related to BLDC/PMSM motors but are designed to run in open-loop step control rather than continuous commutation.

A stepper motor is an electric motor that moves in small, precise steps instead of spinning continuously like a normal motor. Each electrical pulse sent to the motor causes the shaft to rotate by a fixed angle called a step. By sending a specific number of pulses, a controller can move the motor to an exact position. Stepper motors have multiple coils in the stator and a magnetic rotor. The coils are energized in a sequence so the rotor moves from one position to the next. Because the position corresponds to the number of pulses sent, they are often used without feedback sensors. They are commonly used in devices that need accurate positioning, such as 3D printers, CNC machines, and robotics.
