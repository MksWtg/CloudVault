Prerequisites: [[Electric Charge]], [[Electricity#Static Electricity]]

Electrostatic induction is the process where a charged object redistributes charges in a nearby conductor without direct contact.

![[Pasted image 20260309130644.png]]
> Image of redistribution of charges

It’s called “static” electricity because the charges are not moving (through a conductor, doing work).

1) A nearby charge repels or attracts electrons in the conductor.
2) This creates a region of opposite charge near the object and a region of like charge farther away.
3) No electrons are added or removed; they just shift positions within the material.
