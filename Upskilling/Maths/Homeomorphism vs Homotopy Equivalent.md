
Two ways of saying spaces are equivalent. Homeomorphism is stronger.

Consider the disk $D^1$. This is homotopy equivalent to the single point $p$. Because there is a map $f$ from $D^1$ to $p$ that sends every point to $p$. And $g$ sends $p$ to $x_0 \in D^1$.

$g \circ f$ sends every point in $D_1$ to $x_0$ and this is homotopic with $Id_D$. 

$f \circ g$ is $Id_p$ and so the two are obviously homotopic as there is always a homotopy from a path to itself, the trivial homotopy.

But these two spaces are not homeomorphic, there is no continuous map that has an inverse between them.
