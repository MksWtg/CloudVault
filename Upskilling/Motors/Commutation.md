Prerequisites: [[DC Motor]]

Commutation is the process of **switching current through a motor’s windings in the correct sequence** to produce continuous rotation. In DC and BLDC motors, it ensures the rotor always experiences a magnetic field that pulls it forward, creating torque.

In AC motors, commutation happens automatically because the supply voltage **alternates sinusoidally**, so the current in each winding naturally changes direction over time. In other words, the rotating magnetic field is generated directly by the AC waveform, so no mechanical or electronic switching is needed.
