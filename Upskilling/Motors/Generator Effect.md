The Generator Effect is the process by which moving a conductor in a magnetic field (or changing the magnetic field around a conductor) produces an electric current.

It’s how generators produce electricity.

## Explanation

When a wire or coil moves in a magnetic field, the magnetic flux through the conductor changes.

This changing flux induces a current in the conductor, according to Faraday's Law of Induction.

The direction of the induced current is given by Lenz's Law.
