The motor effect is the phenomenon where a physical mechanical force is exerted on a current-carrying conductor placed in a magnetic field. This force is caused by the interaction between the magnetic field produced by the electric current and the external magnetic field.

![[Pasted image 20260301203202.png]]

In the image you can see there is a current travelling in the direction of the wire in the presence of an external magnetic field.

The conductor moves perpendicular to the magnetic field because the magnetic force on moving charges is given by $F=qv×B$. A cross product always produces a direction that is at right angles to both the velocity of the charges (the current direction) and the magnetic field.
