Prerequisites: [[Magnetic Field]], [[Current]]

An eddy current is a circulating electric current induced within a conductor when it is exposed to a changing magnetic field. These currents flow in loops perpendicular to the magnetic field and create their own magnetic field that opposes the change that produced them. Eddy currents can cause energy losses in the form of heat in motors, transformers, and metal structures, but they can also be used intentionally in applications like induction heating, electromagnetic braking, and metal detectors.
