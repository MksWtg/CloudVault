A DC motor works by using the motor effect to turn electrical energy into mechanical motion.

When current flows through a coil of wire placed inside a magnetic field, each side of the coil experiences a magnetic force. In

Because the currents in opposite sides of the coil flow in opposite directions, the forces act in opposite directions too — creating a turning effect (torque) that makes the coil rotate.

A component called a commutator reverses the direction of the current every half turn. This keeps the forces acting in a way that continues the rotation in the same direction, allowing the motor to spin continuously.
