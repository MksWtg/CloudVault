Prerequisites: [[DC Motor]], [[Back EMF]] 

In motors, the resistance is very low (just wire). So when we apply a voltage, how come the current doesn't spike and melt everything?

This is because as the rotor spins up, the coils rotating act like an inductor. This generates a back EMF that opposes the forward EMF. This lowers the voltage. Since V = IR, I = V / R, as V lowers the I lowers as well.

> Although the motor increases to constant speed, the rotation is still a change of flux and generates back EMF

> The current will be high at the start while net EMF is high. This is called inrush current and is unavoidable.
