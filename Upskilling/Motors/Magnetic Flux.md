Magnetic flux is a measure of how much magnetic field passes through a given area.

It depends on three things: the strength of the magnetic field, the size of the area, and the angle between the field and the surface. If the field goes straight through the surface, the flux is maximum; if it runs parallel to the surface, the flux is zero.
