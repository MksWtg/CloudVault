Prerequisites: [[EMF]], [[Voltage]], [[Electromagnetic Induction]], [[Inductance]]

Back EMF (back electromotive force) is the voltage generated by an inductor that opposes the change in current flowing through it. This happens because of Electromagnetic Induction.

This behavior follows Lenz's Law, discovered by Heinrich Lenz, which states:

The induced voltage always acts in a direction that opposes the change that created it.

When current through an Inductor changes:

1) The magnetic field around the coil changes.
2) That changing magnetic field induces a voltage. The induced voltage opposes the change in current. This induced opposing voltage is back EMF.
