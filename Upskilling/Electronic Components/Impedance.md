Impedance is the total opposition a circuit gives to alternating current (AC).

It is like resistance, but it also includes effects from capacitors and inductors, which only matter when current is changing (AC).

Resistance alone only describes opposition in DC circuits. But in AC circuits, current and voltage can shift in time because capacitors and inductors store and release energy. Impedance includes all of that.

$$Z = R + jX$$

Where 

$Z$ is impedance
$R$ is resistance
$j$ is $\sqrt{-1}$
$X$ is reactance

## Why is Impedance Complex

Impedance is expressed as a complex number because it must represent two distinct, simultaneous characteristics in AC circuits: resistance that limits current, and reactance that shifts the phase between voltage and current. Complex numbers allow engineers to calculate both magnitude and phase shift easily using $j$ to represent a $90 \degree$ phase rotation.
