
Capacitance is a measure of a component’s ability to store electrical charge. It tells you how much charge a device can hold for a given voltage. The unit of capacitance is the farad (F), where one farad means one coulomb of charge is stored per volt of potential difference.

$$C = \frac{Q}{V}$$

Capacitance = charge per volts
