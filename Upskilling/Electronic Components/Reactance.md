Reactance is a concept in AC (alternating current) circuits that describes the opposition a component offers to the flow of alternating current due to its inductance or capacitance. it’s like resistance, but specifically for AC and dependent on frequency.

Inductive reactance increases with frequency

$$X_L = 2 \pi f L$$

Capacitive reactance decreases with frequency

$$X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi fC}$$

## Lagging and Leading Current Vs Voltage

Inductors: current lags voltage
Capacitors: current leads voltage

For inductors, when you apply an AC voltage, the inductor resists changes in current because of its inductance. The current cannot increase instantly; it takes time to build up as the magnetic field grows. This means the current reaches its peak later than the voltage. So we say current lags voltage.

For capacitors, when you apply AC voltage, the capacitor starts charging immediately, creating a current even before the voltage across it has fully built up. The current reaches its peak before the voltage does. So we say current leads voltage.
