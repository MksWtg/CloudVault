A diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow in only one direction.

In some electronics, an interruption of current can induce a voltage in the opposite direction. Diodes prevent this voltage from harming other components.

## How Does it Work?

A diode works by allowing electric current to flow in one direction only because of its p-n junction.

![[Pasted image 20260308145014.png]]

A diode is made from a p-type semiconductor (has holes as positive charge carriers) and an n-type semiconductor (has free electrons as negative charge carriers) joined together to form a p-n junction.


At the junction where they meet, some electrons from the n-side fill holes on the p-side, creating a depletion region: an area with no free charge carriers. This is like an insulator.
