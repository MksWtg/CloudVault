## Coupling

A coupling capacitor passes AC signals from one stage to another while blocking DC. It allows only AC voltage variations to reach the next stage and is typically used in audio circuits or amplifiers. For example, a 10 µF capacitor between amplifier stages passes audio signals but blocks DC offset.

## Decoupling/Bypass

Prerequisites: [[Shunting]], [[Noise]]

A decoupling/bypass capacitor shunt high frequency noise.

You would call it a decoupling cap if it focuses on isolating one part of a circuit from voltage fluctuations caused by other parts.

## Bulk


A bulk capacitor is a capacitor used in electronic circuits to store a large amount of energy and supply it when needed, especially to smooth out low-frequency voltage variations in a power supply.
