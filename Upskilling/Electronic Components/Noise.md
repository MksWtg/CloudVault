In electronics, noise refers to unwanted electrical signals that interfere with the desired signal. It is essentially any random or unpredictable variation in voltage or current that can distort or obscure the information you are trying to transmit or measure.

## High Frequency

High-Frequency Noise

- Consists of rapid, fast-changing fluctuations in voltage or current.
- Typically in the kHz to GHz range.

Sources:

- Switching power supplies
- Digital circuits (clock signals, microcontrollers)
- RF interference (radio transmitters, wireless devices)

## Low Frequency

Low-Frequency Noise

- Slower fluctuations in voltage or current, often below 1 kHz.

Sources:

- Flicker noise (1/f noise) in semiconductors
- Mechanical vibrations or microphonic effects
- Power line hum (50/60 Hz)
