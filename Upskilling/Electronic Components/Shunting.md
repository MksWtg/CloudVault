Shunting in electrical circuits refers to providing an alternate path for current to flow, usually to bypass a component or to measure current safely.

A shunt is a low-resistance conductor connected in parallel with another component.

It allows most of the current to “bypass” the component it’s connected across.

Common purpose: to protect sensitive devices from high current.

## Example

If you have a delicate ammeter, connecting it directly to a high-current circuit could damage it.

A shunt resistor is placed in parallel. Most current flows through the shunt, and only a small safe portion flows through the meter.
