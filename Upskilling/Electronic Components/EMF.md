Prerequisites: [[Voltage]], [[Battery]], [[Electric Potential Energy]], [[Circuit]]

Electromotive Force (EMF) is a property of a source of electricity, such as a battery or generator.

EMF is the energy per unit charge (V) supplied by the source.

It is the potential difference created by the source.

> EMF is the voltage when no current is flowing, the circuit is open. When the current is flowing, the terminal voltage might be less due to internal resistance inside the battery. The resistance uses up energy per coulomb, so the voltage across terminals drops.
