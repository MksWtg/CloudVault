Prerequisites: [[Electromagnetic Induction]]

When current flows through a wire, it creates a magnetic field around the wire.
If the current changes, the magnetic field also changes, and this changing field induces a voltage that opposes the change in current. This behavior is called Electromagnetic Induction, first discovered by Michael Faraday.

Inductance (L) measures how strong this effect is.
The unit of inductance is the Henry (H), named after Joseph Henry.
Components designed to have inductance are called Inductors.

Formula:

$$V = L\frac{dI}{dt}$$
Where:

$V$ is the induced voltage
$L$ is inductance
$\frac{dI}{dt}$ is the rate of change of current
