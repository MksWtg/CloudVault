Inductance is a property of an electrical component (usually a coil of wire) that makes it oppose changes in electric current flowing through it. It does this by creating a magnetic field when current flows.

When current in a wire changes, the magnetic field around the wire also changes. That changing magnetic field produces a voltage that pushes back against the change in current. This effect is called inductance.
