Prerequisites: [[Atom]], [[Electricity]]


A battery is a device that stores chemical energy and converts it into electrical energy to provide a voltage (potential difference) across two exposed points called terminals.

More electrons at negative terminal = negative terminal has negative charge = lower electric potential/less volts

Less electrons at `+` terminal = `+` terminal has `+` charge = higher electric potential/more volts

Conventional current flows from positive to negative terminal, from the more volts to less volts side.

Electrons go the other way, from the `-` to the `+`.
