Prerequisites: [[Electronic Components]], [[IC]]

An MCU is a Microcontroller Unit (a type of IC), which is a small computer on a single chip designed to control electronic devices. It combines a processor, memory, and input/output peripherals all in one integrated package.

## Difference from a regular microprocessor

Microprocessors (like those in PCs) usually need external memory and peripherals to work.

MCUs are self-contained, designed for control tasks, and are low-power and inexpensive.
