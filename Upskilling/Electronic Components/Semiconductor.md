A semiconductor is a material whose electrical conductivity is between that of a conductor and an insulator. It can conduct electricity under certain conditions, but not as freely as metals.

Common semiconductors include silicon and germanium.

Pure semiconductors have moderate conductivity that increases with temperature.

Conductivity can be controlled by adding small amounts of impurities, called doping.
