Prerequisites: [[Current]], [[Magnetic Field]]

An electromagnet is a device that produces a magnetic field when an electric current flows through it, usually a coil of wire. It is controlled electrically and can be switched off.

>Note: Magnetism comes from current, not the material itself. It can be made stronger by increasing the current or reversed by changing the direction the current flows in.

The strength of an electromagnet can be increased by coiling the wire, and can be increased even more by placing an a soft iron core inside it.
