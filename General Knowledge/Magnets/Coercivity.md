Prerequisites: [[Magnetization]], [[Ferromagnetism]]

Coercivity (Hc) is the amount of reverse magnetic field needed to bring the net magnetization of a ferromagnet back to zero after it has been magnetized.

It measures how resistant a material is to demagnetization.

High coercivity implies the material is a "hard" ferromagnet. It keeps magnetization and is good for permanent magnets.

Low coercivity implies the material is a "soft" ferromagnet. It is easily demagnetized so it is good for transformers or electromagnets.
