Magnetic domains are microscopic, uniformly magnetized regions within ferromagnetic materials (like iron, nickel, cobalt) where atomic magnetic moments are aligned in the same direction.

![[Pasted image 20260301164913.png]]

These act as "tiny magnets". In unmagnetized materials, domains are randomly oriented, but they align in an external field, creating a permanent magnet.
