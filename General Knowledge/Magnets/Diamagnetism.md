Prerequisites: [[Magnets]], [[Magnetic Field]], [[Magnetic Moment]]

In diamagnetic materials the atomic magnetic moments cancel out, but a tiny magnetic moment is induced in the opposite direction of an applied field.

Effect: Weak repulsion

Diamagnetic materials are substances that create an induced magnetic field in the opposite direction of an applied magnetic field, causing them to be weakly repelled by magnets.

E.g. Pyrolytic Carbon, Bismuth, Copper, Water
