Materials are not magnetic by default. This means they cannot be moved by magnets/magnetic fields.
- every atom has a small magnetic moment
- they point randomly
- they cancel out
## Ferromagnets

Ferromagnets have spontaneous magnetization and can become permanent magnets.

Ferromagnets (nickel, iron, cobalt) do have magnetic moments and can be moved in a magnetic field. 
- atomic magnetic moments point in the same direction (because at the quantum level they experience a mechanical effect called the exchange interaction). This lowers the system's energy when the spins align. So quantum forces between electrons align the magnetic moments
- Even then, the whole material is not aligned it is broken into sections called magnetic domains between which directions differ. So by default, ferromagnets do not have their own field.
	- When the material is magnetized, domains grow or rotate and the magnet becomes permanent.

## Magnetization

- Applying an external magnetic field causes all domains to point in the same direction. Now the ferromagnet produces a magnetic field.
- If the ferromagnet has high coercivity, most domains stay aligned after removing the field. It resists demagnetization. This memory effect is called hysteresis and enables permanent magnets.

## Paramagnetic

- The temperature at which a ferromagnetic material loses its spontaneous magnetization and becomes paramagnetic is called the curie temperature (Tc)
- Below Tc: atomic magnetic moments inside domains stay aligned --> material can be magnetized or stay magnetized
- Above: thermal motion overcomes the exchange interaction, so moments randomize, so net magnetic field disappears.
