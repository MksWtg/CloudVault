Prerequisites: [[Magnets]], [[Magnetic Field]], [[Magnetic Moment]]

Materials are not magnetic by default. This means they cannot be moved by magnets/magnetic fields.
- every atom has a small magnetic moment
- they point randomly
- they cancel out

Ferromagnets (nickel, iron, cobalt) do have magnetic moments and can act in a magnetic field. 
- atomic magnetic moments point in the same direction (because at the quantum level they experience a mechanical effect called the exchange interaction). This lowers the system's energy when the spins align. So quantum forces between electrons align the magnetic moments
- Even then, the whole material is not aligned it is broken into sections called magnetic domains between which directions differ. When the material is magnetized, domains grow or rotate and the magnet becomes permanent.
- Ferromagnets stay magnetized after the external field is removed. It resists demagnetization. This memory effect is called hysteresis and enables permanent magnets.
