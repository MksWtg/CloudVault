

- Paramagnetism is where materials are weakly attracted to an externally applied magnetic field and form internal, induced magnetic fields in the direction of the applied field (e.g. Oxygen, Lithium)
- The temperature at which a ferromagnetic material loses its spontaneous magnetization and becomes paramagnetic is called the curie temperature (Tc)
- Below Tc: atomic magnetic moments inside domains stay aligned --> material can be magnetized or stay magnetized
- Above: thermal motion overcomes the exchange interaction, so moments randomize, so net magnetic field disappears.
