Prerequisites: [[Magnetic Field]], [[Retentivity]], [[Coercivity]]

Are they correlated? Often, yes: materials with high retentivity tend to have high coercivity, because strong domain alignment usually comes with strong resistance to demagnetization.

But not always correlated. Counterexamples:
Alnico magnets have high retentivity, but coercivity is moderate. They can lose magnetization under strong reverse fields.

Ferrites have lower retentivity but very high coercivity- very stable, but weaker field.
