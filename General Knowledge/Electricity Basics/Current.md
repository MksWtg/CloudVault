Current measures how much charge passes a point per unit time in a circuit. The current of a circuit depends on 

In metals, this is usually electrons moving, while in solutions or plasmas, it can be ions moving.

Unit: ampere (A)
- 1 A = 1 coulomb of charge passing a point per second.

Formula: $$I = \frac{Q}{t}$$
$I$ = current
$Q$ = charge
$t$ = time
