Prerequisites: [[Electric Charge]], [[Electric Field]], [[Energy]]

Electric potential energy is the amount of (electric) potential energy a unit charge would have at a specific point in an electric field.

In simple terms, it tells you how much “push” or energy a charge would have at a location, even if it’s not moving.

It only makes sense to measure electric potential relative to another point, e.g. the ground (earth) or the other terminal of the battery.
